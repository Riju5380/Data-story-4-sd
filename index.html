The Data Story

The scale of Aadhaar in India is massive. By March 2025, the Unique Identification Authority of India had issued more than 1.41 billion Aadhaar numbers, reaching about 94.7% of the population. In most states and union territories, coverage is above 90%, showing how widely Aadhaar has spread across the country.

This wide coverage has helped build the JAM system—Jan Dhan, Aadhaar, and Mobile—which connects identity with banking and mobile access. Because of this, bank account ownership in India increased from 17% in 2009 to 77% in 2024. Today, around 86.8 crore Aadhaar numbers are linked to bank accounts, making it easier for people to receive money directly.

Aadhaar has also changed how welfare schemes work. Through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT), the government has been able to remove around 100 million fake beneficiaries, saving ₹3.72 lakh crore by March 2025.
